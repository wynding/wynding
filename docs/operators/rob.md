# Operator Profile — Rob (Maintainer)

> **Scope:** This is the maintainer's personal operating profile — how _Rob_ works and how
> agents should collaborate with him specifically. It is **not binding on contributors.** If
> you're a regular contributor (human or agent), you're welcome to add your own
> `docs/operators/<name>.md` and work the way that suits you, within the project's
> [engineering principles](../engineering-principles.md) and
> [working agreement](../working-agreement.md).

## Role

Rob is the **product owner**: he sets the vision, prioritizes features, and holds the 🔴
Owner calls in the [working agreement](../working-agreement.md) — product/creative rules,
balance, and external/irreversible actions.

## Working with Rob

- **He's not a strong developer, by his own description — and that's the point of an
  AI-first project.** Delegate technical implementation _entirely_. Never ask him whether
  something should be a singleton, which data structure to use, or how to lay out files.
  Bring him decisions, not mechanics.
- **Architecture is co-owned.** Surface it as _product-legible_ tradeoffs — cost, lock-in,
  longevity, moddability, platform reach — with a clear recommendation. He'll weigh in on
  those; he's not auditing the code.
- **Player-facing choices are generally his**, but make the low-stakes, reversible ones
  yourself and follow up after (🟡 Notify) — he isn't a UX specialist and would rather you
  move.
- **Bias to autonomy** on anything reversible and cheap-if-wrong. He'd rather correct
  occasionally than be pinged constantly.

## What Rob values in the work

These are his personal lean toward the project's [engineering
principles](../engineering-principles.md) — expect him to hold them warmly:

- **Automate all the things** — propose automation for anything repetitive; he'll almost
  always want it (his bar: automate unless it's genuinely one-time).
- **Improving the work beats doing the work** — surface process/tooling improvements as you
  go, don't just grind the task.
- **No silent corner-cuts** — flag any shortcut explicitly; he'd rather build it right.
- **Continuous improvement** — set a goal, iterate toward it, keep looking for the next
  improvement in both the work and how we do it.
